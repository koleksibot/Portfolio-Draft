My Learning Journey
Why I Chose to Self Learn
I created a pros and cons list and determined that self learning is the best option.


Pros:
  • I have the power to choose what I learn and when I learn it.

  • I can go at my own pace, and tailor the curriculum to my interests. I never have to waste my "learning energy" and take courses I do not need.

  • I want to keep the passion of designing and coding alive. I've pursued academically intensive programs before and the stressful, lecture-based structure can suck the fun out of learning. I learn best with a practical approach; Learning through projects.

  • Financially viable option
My Review of Self Learning (so far)
At first, I was overwhelmed by the sheer amount of topics I needed to cover, and often felt discouraged when I ran into errors. Over time, I learned that running into errors and debugging are an essential part of the coding process. I learned to systematically deal with my problems, and used various resources (*cough* special thanks to Stack Overflow) to find solutions.
The Challenges
Self learning is challenging. I often ran into difficulties like finding the motivation or drive to learn, optimizing the use of my time, and finding structure within my learning. Imagine a person who knows nothing about coding whatsoever. This person has to know exactly what topics to learn and find just the right resources to learn them.
